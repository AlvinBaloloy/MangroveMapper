🧪 About the Mangrove Vegetation Index (MVI)

The Mangrove Vegetation Index (MVI) uses three Sentinel-2 bands to distinguish mangrove forests from terrestrial vegetation based on greenness and moisture.

Formula:

MVI = |NIR – Green| / |SWIR – Green|

Where:

  • NIR = Near Infrared (Band 8)
  • Green = Visible Green (Band 3)
  • SWIR = Shortwave Infrared (Band 11)

🌿 How It Works

  • |NIR – Green| captures greenness contrast between mangroves and terrestrial vegetation.
  • |SWIR – Green| represents moisture unique to mangroves, without using tidal indices.
  • Higher MVI = higher probability of mangrove classification.

🌍 Applications

  • Global mangrove extent mapping
  • Change detection (2019–present)
  • Conservation planning
  • Coastal resource management

Used by: FAO, The Nature Conservancy, and academic institutions.
